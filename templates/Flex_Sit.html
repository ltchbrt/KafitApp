SIT AND REACH

- to test the flexibility of the lower extremities (particularly the trunk).

EQUIPMENT

* Tape measure or meter stick

Procedure

For the Performer:

* Sit on the floor with back, head and shoulders flat on the wall. Feet should be 12 inches apart.

* Interlock thumbs and position the tip of the fingers on the floor without bending the elbows.

* After the partner has positioned the zero point of the tape measure the pupil/student starts the test by sliding the hands slowly forward without jerking, trying to reach the farthest point possible without bending the knees.

* Bouncing or jerking movement is not allowed.

* Do it twice.

For the Partner:

* As the performer assumes the (b) procedure, position the zero point of the tape measure 15 inches away from the heel of the performer.

* See to it that the knees are not bent as the performer slides the farthest distance that he could.

* Record farthest distance reached in centimeters.

SCORING

* record the farthest distance between the two trials to the nearest centimeters.
